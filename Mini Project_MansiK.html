Introduction to the Data Set

Two different data sets are used to support this data visualization project. Both are acquired from Kaggle, an online data science community where users can find and posts data sets. The following are the two data sets that are used :

  1. Web Series : Ultimate Collection curated by Amritvir Singh.
    The data was scrapped from IMDb, an online entertainment information database. It includes '9 columns', or what is called as 'Variables'. It has information such as the name of the series, the year the title was released, content rating, IMDb ratings, Rotten Tomato ratings, genre, number of seasons the series has, description and the platform its currently streaming on. Each variable has around '12,000 rows' or 'Observations'. For the purpose of this project we will be using just the title and the ratings variable and its observations from this data set.

  2. The Office Dataset curated by Neha Prabhavalkar.
    This data was also scrapped from IMDb. It's limited to the series itself and includes '12 Variables' and '188 unique Observations'. The variables are catagorized into seasons, episode title, description, individual episode ratings, votes and viewership count in USA for the said episode, guest star name (if any), duration of the episode, directors and the writers names. This data set would be used to explore more of the behind-the-scene questions I have about the series.

The Office vs. other series

In this section we will compare The Office to 19 other popular TV shows in terms of IMDb ranking, both from a mix genre category and from comedy genre category.
Lets start with the mix genre category...

Where does The Office stand in terms of ranking with 19 other popular TV shows?

To visualize this, we will be using a simple horizontal bar graph. This diagram would be the most appropriate for this question as we are only concerned with 2 variables, the title and the overall IMDb ranking for the series.

In the visualization above, the x-axis encodes the ratings for titles present in the y-axis. The 19 other tv shows are curated from an online top 20 list of must watch TV shows. When we compare The office with these 19 popular TV shows, it ranks 5th sharing its place with Friends (1994), with 8.9/10 ratings on IMDb. The first 4 top rated series are as follows - Breaking Bad, Game of thrones, The Sopranos and Sherlock.


Where does The Office stand in terms of ranking with 19 other popular Comedy TV shows?

Since this question is similar to the one before, we would use a horizontal bar graph for this too. Again the variables are same as above with the only difference being the title having the same genre i.e. Comedy. The other 19 best comedy tv shows of all time is curated from The New York Times 21 best comedies of the 21st Century.

In the Comedy category the Office ranks first with 8.9 IMDb ratings. A similar mockumentary called "Parks and Recreation" ranks 4th from top with a rating of 8.6/10.

In conclusion, I think its safe to say that The Office really is the most amazing (and Top rated) comedy series of all time.


Deep dive into The Office

In this sections we will try to explore patterns in the viewership count in USA, Impact of celebrity appearance (if any) and find out how many of the actors also wrote and directed the episodes in the series.

The Office viewer trend in the US.

To better understand the Viewership count in the US and explore any patterns in the count. The most appropriate visualization type for this would be a line chart.

The average viewership for season 1 was 6.4 million. As the season progresses, the viewer count picks up reaching over 8.2 million viewers tuning in to watch the TV show. The upward trend continues till season 5, it being the most watched season having almost 8.8 million viewer count. It was also the longest running season out of the 9, consisting a total of 28 episodes.

After season 5 the graph follows a downward trend. By season 7 the average viewers dropped by 1.5 million reaching 7.3 million. After season 7 the count drops drastically reaching 5.4 million, lower than season 1. Season 7 is also the season where Steve Carell a.k.a Micheal Scott leaves the Office due to expiration of the contract. His absence can be one of the factor for the drop in viewers, him being the important central character in the series.
By season 9, the viewership had reached the lowest of average viewer count of around 4.2 million.


Looking at celebrity appearances and their influence on viewership and ratings

Like any popular series, The Office had a fair share of well known celebrity appearances.

The visualization above is called a matrix plot. The squares on the matrix is known as a square mark. The color and the size of the mark encodes the rating for the episode and the average viewercount respectively.

Amy Adams appeared twice in the show i.e. in episode 5 and episode 9. According to the matrix plot, episode number 77 with Jack Black, Jessica Alba and Cloris Leachman had a total of around 23 million viewer count and was also the most rated (9.8) of the lot.


Actors who not only acted on the show but directed and wrote some of the episodes

One of the most interesting fact that I came across while watching the Office was finding out many of the actors from the series, along with acting also wrote and directed a number of episodes. I have been curious ever since to find out who among the cast wrote and directed most of the episodes. To analyse this, the visualization we will be using is the scatter plot.

The Scatter plot has x-axis representing the number of episodes that the cast member has written and y-axis representing the number of episodes that the cast member has directed. The coor of the circle denotes the actors name and the number below the circles on the plot denotes the total number of episodes the actor has appeared in.

In conclusion, Mindy Kaling has written and directed most episodes out of the cast, while also acting in the series and Paul Lieberstein has directed most of the episodes amongst the lot.

John Krasinski and Rainn Wilson both have directed 2 episodes each while acting in all 201 episode.

Four of the regularly appearing secondary characters have the same first name as the actress or actor who plays her or him: Angela (Angela Kinsey), Oscar (Oscar Nuñez), Phyllis (Phyllis Smith), and Creed (Creed Bratton).
